PADI Open Water Diver

Three days from your first breath in our pool to a certification card that works anywhere on earth. Never more than four of you per instructor, taught at your pace, and you'll leave knowing how to dive a reef without leaving a trace on it.

What the three days look like

Day 1 · Theory & pool

Morning theory over coffee (or done at home beforehand with PADI eLearning), then into our private pool. Mask, regulator, buoyancy: every skill at your pace before you see the ocean.

Day 2 · First ocean dives

Two shore dives at Jemeluk Bay, our calm house reef just along the shore. You'll repeat the pool skills in the sea and spend the rest of the dive just… diving.

Day 3 · Dive deeper, go further

Two more dives to a maximum of 18 metres, usually the Pyramids or Melasti reef. Final theory review, and you walk out a certified diver.

Do I qualify?

You need to be 10 or older, in reasonable health, and able to swim 200 metres (any style, no time limit) and float for 10 minutes. That's it. If you have asthma, heart conditions, or take regular medication, message us first and we'll check the medical form together before you book anything.

Questions divers actually ask

I'm nervous / I tried once and panicked. Is this for me?

This is exactly what the private pool is for. You'll do every skill standing-depth first, repeat anything as many times as you need, and nobody moves to the ocean until you're comfortable. Small groups mean nobody is rushing you.

Can I do the theory before I arrive?

Yes: PADI eLearning lets you finish all the theory at home, which turns day one into mostly pool time. Ask us on WhatsApp and we'll send the enrolment link.

What about the Liberty wreck?

The wreck sits at 5–30 metres, so you'll touch the top of it as an Open Water diver, but it's the main reason most people continue straight into the Advanced course. Our Open Water + Advanced pack (IDR 9.900.000) is built for exactly that.

When is the best time to dive Amed?

Amed dives year-round. April to November brings the best visibility (20 m+); the water is 26–29 °C whatever the month.
