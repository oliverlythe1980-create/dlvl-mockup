Lipah Bay

A small black-sand bay of coral bommies, relaxed and shallow, and a reef-shark nursing site, as good for snorkellers as divers.

The Site

Lipah is a small, sheltered black-sand bay scattered with coral bommies on sand, relaxed, shallow and easy. It’s a known reef-shark nursing site, so juvenile blacktips are sometimes seen in the shallows.

A classic easy second dive and a lovely spot for snorkellers, sheltered from the strong currents of the Lombok Strait.

What You’ll See

  • Coral bommies dotted across the sand
  • Juvenile reef sharks in the nursery shallows
  • Anthias, parrotfish and angelfish over the coral
  • Macro on the bommies: nudibranchs, shrimp, gobies

Conditions & When to Dive It

Sheltered, shallow and calm, suitable for all levels and snorkellers. Gentle currents. Visibility 10–25 metres; warm year-round.

How We Dive It

We dive Lipah as a relaxed second dive or an easy day, ideal after something deeper. Four divers maximum, a gentle pace, and time to enjoy the bommies.

Good to Know

Are the sharks dangerous?

No, these are small, shy juvenile reef sharks using the bay as a nursery. A privilege to see.

Good for snorkelling?

Yes, it’s shallow and sheltered, lovely from the surface.
