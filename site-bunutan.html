Bunutan

A sandy point on the Amed strip with garden-eel colonies and a gentle deep slope, often dived as an easy drift.

The Site

Bunutan Point begins with a gentle sandy slope dotted with macro life and garden-eel colonies, then deepens into open water often swept by a soft current, which makes it a lovely easy drift.

Quietly excellent and usually empty, it’s a favourite for divers who like to fly along a slope and let the reef come to them.

What You’ll See

  • Garden-eel colonies swaying on the sand
  • Nudibranchs and macro critters on the slope
  • Schooling fish, barracuda and the occasional reef shark deeper down
  • Rays and flatfish on the sand

Conditions & When to Dive It

Suits all levels as a relaxed drift, with deeper sections for Advanced divers. Currents are usually gentle. Visibility 10–30 metres.

How We Dive It

We run Bunutan as an easy drift, letting the current do the work while we point out the macro and watch the blue. Four divers maximum, a relaxed pace.

Good to Know

Is it a drift dive?

Usually a gentle one, yes, an easy, enjoyable way to cover the slope.

Good for all levels?

Yes, as a relaxed drift it suits everyone, with deeper sections for Advanced divers who want them.
