Where You’ll Dive

The Amed–Tulamben coast is a string of world-class shore dives, a WWII wreck, coral bays, walls and artificial reefs, all entered from black-sand beaches along one short coastline. These are the sites we dive daily, know intimately, and help look after.

The Coast at a Glance

Eight sites along one volcanic shoreline, from the Liberty in Tulamben to the Japanese Wreck at Banyuning. Tap a pin to read its chapter in the guide.

Tulamben · 25 min by car

USAT Liberty Wreck

A 120-metre WWII cargo ship, torpedoed in 1942 and pushed into the sea by Mount Agung’s 1963 eruption. Now it’s the most famous shore dive on earth: walk in off the black sand, and within minutes you’re drifting along a coral-crusted hull. Come at dawn and the resident school of bumphead parrotfish files past like commuters.

5–30 mdepth
All levelsfrom Open Water

Amed · our house reef

Jemeluk Bay

Calm, shallow, and absurdly generous, this is where every Open Water student takes their first reef breath, and where we keep coming back on days off. Coral gardens from three metres, a wall that drops away for the deeper dives, and the kind of macro life that turns a 45-minute dive into 70.

Banyuning · 10 min by car

Japanese Wreck

A small patrol boat from WWII, sitting so shallow that snorkellers can see it, but its best secrets are for divers. The hull is completely overgrown with soft coral, and the surrounding slope hides pygmy seahorses for those with patient eyes and good buoyancy. An underrated favourite.

Amed · 5 minutes away

The Pyramids

Artificial reef structures stacked on the sand like sunken temples, now swarming with glassfish, lionfish and the occasional passing turtle. A brilliant second dive, a perfect navigation classroom, and proof that given a little help, the ocean rebuilds fast.

All levelsgreat second dive

Tulamben · 25 min by car

Coral Garden

Tulamben’s shallow sculpture garden: statues and structures laid among soft coral, all within easy reach of the surface. A photographer’s playground, a gentle second dive, and the prettiest safety stop on the coast.

Tulamben · 25 min by car

Tulamben Drop-Off

Where the lava rock falls away into deep blue. Giant fans, schooling fish along the wall, and the occasional something-big cruising past out of the depths. This is the classroom for our deep courses, and a favourite of every visiting divemaster.

Amed · 500 m from our door

Melasti Reef

Our neighbourhood dive, literally down the path from the bungalows. A sloping coral reef with seagrass patches and resident turtles, perfect for an easy morning dive or a relaxed afternoon when you can’t face the drive anywhere else.

Seraya · 20 min by car

Seraya Secrets

Black sand and tiny treasures. Seraya is the muck-diving icon of the coast: frogfish, harlequin shrimp, ghost pipefish, and nudibranchs by the dozen. Bring a torch, slow right down, and let Irman’s biologist eyes find you things you’d swim straight past.

5–20 mdepth
