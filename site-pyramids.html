The Pyramids

Stacked concrete reef structures on the sand off Amed, two decades grown and swarming with glassfish, with turtles passing through.

The Site

The Pyramids are a series of concrete cube blocks stacked into pyramid shapes on a sandy bottom off Amed beach, a few minutes along the coast from Jemeluk. Seeded two decades ago, they’ve grown into a thriving artificial reef and proof that, given a little help, the ocean rebuilds fast.

It’s a brilliant second dive, often run as a gentle drift, and our favourite classroom for underwater navigation.

What You’ll See

  • Swirling clouds of glassfish around the structures
  • Lionfish, batfish, groupers and reef fish
  • Turtles passing through the sand channels
  • Macro on the blocks: nudibranchs, shrimp, gobies

Conditions & When to Dive It

Shallow and forgiving, suitable for all levels. Often a relaxed drift along the structures. Visibility 10–25 metres; gentle conditions most days.

How We Dive It

We use the Pyramids as a relaxed second dive and our navigation classroom for the Advanced course. Four divers maximum, an easy pace, and plenty to point at.

Good to Know

Is it a drift dive?

Often a gentle one, depending on the day. We brief it each morning.

Good for new divers?

Yes, it’s shallow and easy, a great confidence-builder after the house reef.
