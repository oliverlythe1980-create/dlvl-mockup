Melasti Reef

Our neighbourhood dive, 500 metres from the door: a gentle slope of coral and seagrass with resident turtles.

The Site

Melasti is the reef at the end of our own path, 500 metres from the dive centre and the beach we’re named after. A gentle slope of coral and seagrass, it’s perfect for an easy morning dive or a relaxed afternoon when you don’t feel like the drive anywhere else.

Quietly lovely, and home to a population of resident turtles.

What You’ll See

  • Resident green turtles grazing the seagrass
  • Sloping coral with reef fish and anthias
  • Octopus, moray eels and macro on the slope
  • Rays and garden eels on the sandy patches

Conditions & When to Dive It

Gentle and shallow, suitable for all levels and the easiest logistics on the coast, you can practically walk to it. Visibility 10–25 metres; calm most days.

How We Dive It

Melasti is our zero-hassle dive: stroll down, gear up, dive. Ideal for a quick second dive, a refresher, or an early morning before the day warms up. Four divers maximum.

Good to Know

How far is it?

500 metres from the dive centre, the easiest dive we run, no drive needed.

Will I see turtles?

There’s a resident population, so very often, yes.
